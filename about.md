# About Me

I'm a college student at Rose-Hulman Institue of Technology studying computer science, math, and cybersecurity. 

Some interests of mine include:
## Cryptology
- I found a senior thesis topic! I'm studying <span class="secondarycolor">deniable encryption</span> & trying to apply it to TLS to then see what the privacy implications are. If my hypothesis is right, TLS rewritten to use deniable cryptographic primitives should be beneficial for user privacy without any work from the user.
- Other interesting topics
	- <span class="secondarycolor">Sudoku based encryption</span> as initially seen [in this arXiv paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.10119). "Sudoku based encryption" already sounds intruiging, and applying it beyond images/video/audio sounds like a fun problem.
	- <span class="secondarycolor">Lattice based encryption</span>, particularly how implementation mistakes could be exploited. If my own experience of implementing [Kangaroo12](https://keccak.team/kangarootwelve.html) holds any weight, its *really* easy to mess up implementation of what's otherwise secure. This could be generalized to just looking at potential implementation flaws in FIPS 203-205.

## Privacy
- It's easy to feel like David against the Goliath that is existing without having all your personal information used and monetized, but I still think personal privacy is something worth caring about. Ironically, I think my main motivation for this would be scouting for my VRC/FRC teams in high school, as this was essentially diet OSINT to hopefully get a slight competitive edge in competitions. More recently, I'm interested in seeing how cryptography can be used to supplement user privacy, ideally without any work on the users end.
